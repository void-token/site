0. How does this work?

This is an experiment. Void Token (VOID) is an Ethereum based token that loses supply when used. Everytime someone transfers some VOID, 5% of the transfer amount is burned and thus the circulating supply becomes smaller. This creates an environment roughly opposite to a traditional currency which has its supply inflated.

1. Why?

Almost all currencies are inflationary, which chips away at the purchasing power (and wealth) of the middle and lower classes especially. Sometimes it leads to total disaster (think Zimbabwe or Venezuala). What would happen if a token worked in the exact opposite way to a traditionally inflated currency? What if the supply was DEFLATED instead of inflated. Would it work at all? Let's find out with this experiment.

2. What are the details?

Void Token started with a supply of 1 million tokens. Everytime a transfer is made, 5% of the tokens transferred are burnt and removed from the circulating supply. This means that instead of the token supply increasing or staying the same over time, it decreases constantly.

3. What are the expectations for this token?

There are none. This is an experiment which is possible thanks to blockchain technology. This token has no metrics that determine success or failure, rather it will simply yeild information regarding what happens when supply is deflated instead of inflated. As future generations reject the inflating away of their hard-earned money, they will look to new monetary ideas and solutions. Any datapoints gathered from previous generations will be helpful in making sure they make wiser and more informed choices.

4. What about other deflationary tokens?

These other tokens are our inspiration, experiements in the same field which should also be explored. $bombtoken and $halflife depreciate at 1% and 2% respectively. Void token depreciates at a hyper-aggressive 5% per transaction, meaning this experiment will simply be another useful datapoint alongside the others.
